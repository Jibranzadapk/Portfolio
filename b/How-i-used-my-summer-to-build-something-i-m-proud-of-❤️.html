How I Used My Summer to Build Something I’m Proud Of ❤️

When my first semester ended, I didn’t want my summer to just fade away doing nothing special. I wanted to create something meaningful — something that would last. That’s how I ended up building my own portfolio website from scratch. It started as a simple idea during my break, but it soon became one of my favorite personal projects.

I wanted to use my free time wisely, so I decided to make a space on the internet that truly reflected me — my skills, interests, and the things I was learning. It wasn’t about creating something huge; I just wanted to build something that felt personal and real.

The plan was clear: keep it clean, minimal, and slightly tech-inspired. I built everything with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, without relying on any pre-made templates or frameworks. I wanted to understand how each part worked — from the layout to the animations — line by line.

My website follows a green-on-black theme, giving it a subtle hacker-style vibe but keeping it modern and simple. It’s responsive, lightweight, and works smoothly across all devices. From the very beginning, I wanted it to feel like a focused digital home — not just another student portfolio.

This journey taught me far more than just web development. It helped me build patience, creativity, and consistency. Spending my summer coding every day gave me confidence and a sense of pride — knowing every detail, from colors to scripts, was created by me.
